Why monitoring terrorism-related web activity matters

Propaganda & recruitment

Extremist groups often misuse websites and social media pages to share propaganda, spread hate, and attract new supporters. Detecting such content early helps limit its reach and impact.

Phishing & illegal funding

Fraudulent donation pages, phishing emails and fake campaigns can be used to collect money under false identities. URL-based analysis helps flag suspicious financial activity that may be linked to terrorism.

Hidden coordination

Encrypted messaging, coded websites and disguised forums can be used for planning and coordination. Analysing patterns in URLs is one of many tools that can assist investigators and security professionals.

Important: FreedomGuard is designed to support awareness and research. It does not replace law-enforcement processes or professional threat intelligence.

How FreedomGuard works

1. Paste the URL

Copy any link from email, chat, or social media and paste it into the analyzer.

2. ML-based inspection

The system extracts key URL features and runs them through trained machine learning models (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, etc.).

3. Get a clear verdict

See if the website is Safe or Unsafe with a donut chart and confidence score so you can decide whether to trust it.

Key Features

Real-time Detection

Instant risk assessment for every URL you check.

ML-Powered

Trained on thousands of phishing and legitimate websites.

Visual Dashboard

Donut charts and progress bars for quick interpretation.

Privacy-Friendly

URLs are only used for analysis inside your session, not stored.

Note: FreedomGuard is a research prototype and should be used as an additional safety tool. For critical actions (online banking, government services, etc.), always verify the website address manually even if it appears safe.
